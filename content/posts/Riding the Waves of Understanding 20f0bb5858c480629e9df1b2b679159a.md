### Understanding Wave Dynamics Through Simulations

Let’s take a fundamental concept in physics: **waves**. For many students, frequency, amplitude, wavelength, and wavenumber remain abstract—confined to textbook diagrams.

Now, imagine a student designing an AI-powered, web-based simulation to explore these very concepts:

- **Frequency**
    - **Definition:** Frequency (f) is the number of complete oscillations (or cycles) a wave undergoes per unit time, typically measured in Hertz ($Hz$).
    - **Simulation Insight:** Students input different frequency values and *see* the wave oscillate faster or slower. The plotted graph visualizes the number of cycles over time, reinforcing conceptual understanding.
- **Amplitude**
    - **Definition:** Amplitude (A) is the maximum displacement from a wave’s equilibrium position—essentially its height or intensity.
    - **Simulation Insight:** Adjusting amplitude inputs makes the wave taller or shorter. This change can be linked to real-world effects like sound loudness or light brightness.
- **Wavelength**
    - **Definition:** Wavelength ($λ$) is the spatial period of a periodic wave—the distance over which the wave pattern repeats (e.g., crest to crest).
    - **Simulation Insight:** Students observe how changing the wavelength affects spacing between crests or troughs, helping them visualize how short wavelengths mean more waves fit in a given space.
- **Wavenumber**
    - **Definition:** Wavenumber ($k$) measures spatial frequency, representing the number of wave cycles per unit distance. It's inversely proportional to wavelength, calculated as $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$
    - **Simulation Insight:** Though more abstract, simulations help students understand wavenumber’s role in wave behavior, especially when modeling electromagnetic or quantum phenomena.

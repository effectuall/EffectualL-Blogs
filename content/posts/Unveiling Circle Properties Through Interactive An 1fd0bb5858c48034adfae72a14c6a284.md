Circles are fundamental shapes in mathematics, appearing everywhere particularly in the design of wheels. While their definitions are simple, truly grasping their properties, especially the relationship between their radius and circumference, can sometimes be challenging with static diagrams.

### **Understanding Key Circle Properties**

1. **Radius $(r)$**
The radius is the distance from the center of the circle to any point on its circumference. It's the fundamental measure that defines the size of the circle.
2. Diameter $(d)$
    
    The diameter is any straight line segment that passes through the center of the circle and has its endpoints on the circumference. It is the longest chord of the circle. The diameter is always twice the radius of the circle.
    
    The mathematical formula for diameter is $d=2r.$
    
3. **Circumference $(C)$**
The circumference is the total distance around the edge of the circle. It's the circular equivalent of the perimeter of a polygon. The animation vividly demonstrates that if you were to "unroll" or "straighten out" the circle's edge, its length would be its circumference.
The mathematical formula for circumference is $C=2\pi r = d \pi$ 
4.  π **(Pi)**: 
    
    A mathematical constant approximately equal to 3.14159. It represents the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.
